Geomagnetic Storms

Climate Change:

Solar winds triggered by geomagnetic storms can have profound effects on Earth's atmosphere and climate. These effects include acceleration of global warming, rise in sea level, and increase in the frequency and severity of extreme weather events. This may cause agricultural lands to be exposed to drought or floods, decrease the fertility of the soil and reduce biodiversity. Agricultural lands, especially in coastal areas, may face problems such as salinization and erosion.
